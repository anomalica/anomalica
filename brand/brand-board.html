Sample Article - Light

2004 USS Nimitz Encounter

Last updated 2026-03-25 · 47 sources · 312 claims

Background

The USS Princeton, an Aegis-class guided-missile cruiser operating as part of the Nimitz carrier strike group, had been tracking anomalous radar contacts for approximately two weeks prior to the visual encounter. According to Senior Chief Kevin Day, the air defence controller aboard the Princeton, groups of objects were observed descending from approximately 8,500 metres to sea level within seconds before hovering and departing at high speed.

Visual Encounter

On 14 November 2004, Commander David Fravor and Lieutenant Commander Alex Dietrich, both pilots of F/A-18F Super Hornets from VFA-41, were directed to investigate one of the contacts. Both pilots reported observing a white, capsule-shaped object approximately 12 metres in length, moving erratically above a disturbance on the ocean surface. The object departed at high speed when Fravor attempted to close distance.

A second flight later that day, piloted by a crew from VFA-41, captured infrared video of a similar object using the aircraft's Forward Looking Infrared system. This footage, later designated the "FLIR1" video, was declassified by the United States Department of Defense in April 2020.

Sources: Fravor, D. (2017) Written Statement to US Senate; Day, K. (2019) Interview, The Nimitz Encounters; Pentagon UAP Task Force (2020) Preliminary Assessment
