# Conditional release (escrow) for sensitive submissions

Date: 2026-03-21
Status: draft

## Context

Some submitters may have material they want released only if something happens to them. This was originally conceived as a "dead man's switch" with heartbeat check-ins, automated timers, and duress codes, but analysis showed that this added complexity without adding value. The share holders themselves are the mechanism - no timers needed.

Research into WikiLeaks' insurance file failure (single encryption key published accidentally by a journalist) demonstrated that single-key systems are fragile. Shamir Secret Sharing (a method for splitting a secret into multiple pieces so that only a specified number of pieces are needed to reconstruct it) eliminates single points of failure by distributing trust.

## Decision

The system will work in three stages:

**1. Upload** - the submitter will encrypt a file in their browser before upload. The system will generate three Shamir shares (2 of 3 required to decrypt). The submitter will receive two shares and Anomalica will keep one. The submitter will give one share to a trusted contact via a printed letter generated by the browser in any of the 30 supported languages.

**2. Wait** - the encrypted document will sit on the platform. Nobody can read it. No timers, no heartbeats, no automated check-ins.

**3. Release** - when any two shares come together, the document will be decrypted and enter the editorial review queue.

| Scenario | Shares combined |
|----------|----------------|
| Submitter changes their mind | Submitter + Anomalica |
| Something happens to submitter | Contact + Anomalica |
| Anomalica ceases operation | Submitter + Contact |

A valid share will be the authorisation. No identity check, no approval process. Each share will be distinct, so the release log will show which shares were used.

Anomalica will not collect the share holder's contact details. The printed letter will instruct the share holder to contact Anomalica and provide their code. At least one external share holder will be required - without one, the platform operator could decrypt at will.

Submissions will have a maximum holding period (default 5 years, renewable). If Anomalica ceases operation, its shares will be published to a distributed file network and the encrypted files distributed.

## Consequences

The system will be simple enough to explain in a three-stage diagram. A non-technical submitter will need to understand: upload, give the envelope to someone you trust, and either submit your share to release early or wait for your contact to act. No technical knowledge required beyond using the website.

Release will mean decryption, not immediate publication. Decrypted content will enter the editorial review queue.
